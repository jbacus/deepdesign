Course Title: Design Thinking

Course Description: This course introduces students to the design thinking process and its application in various fields, including business, product design, and social impact. Students will learn how to identify and understand user needs, brainstorm and prototype solutions, and test and iterate their designs. The course will also explore the ethical and societal implications of design thinking.

Course Goals:

-   Understand the design thinking process and its application in various fields
-   Learn how to identify and understand user needs
-   Develop skills in brainstorming, prototyping, and testing
-   Explore the ethical and societal implications of design thinking

Course Outcomes:

-   Students will be able to apply design thinking to real-world problems
-   Students will develop skills in empathy, problem-solving, and creative thinking
-   Students will be able to prototype and test design solutions
-   Students will gain a deeper understanding of the ethical and societal implications of design thinking

Course Topics:

1.  Introduction to design thinking and its application
2.  Empathy and user research
3.  Defining the problem and generating ideas
4.  Prototyping and testing
5.  Iteration and design implementation
6.  Ethical and societal implications of design thinking
7.  Guest lectures and case studies
8.  Final project presentations

Grading:

-   Participation and class discussions (20%)
-   In-class exercises and homework assignments (30%)
-   Midterm project (25%)
-   Final project (25%)

Prerequisites: None

Textbooks:

-   "Design Thinking" by Tim Brown
-   "The Design Thinking Playbook" by Michael Lewrick, Patrick Link, and Larry Leifer

Note: This is a general syllabus, it's only an example and it might not be suitable for all the classes. The actual syllabus might vary depending on the instructor, the school and the level of the class.
